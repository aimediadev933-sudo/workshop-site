Overview

The "Network and Information Security Workshop" was organized by the Department of IT & Telecom (DITT), Bhutan, with financial support from TEIN*CC and collaboration with Sri Lanka CERT CC and APNIC. This comprehensive five-day workshop was designed to enhance the cybersecurity capabilities of system and network administrators from various Bhutanese institutions.

With Bhutan's growing digital infrastructure, the workshop addressed the increasing cyber risks by equipping participants with both theoretical knowledge and hands-on skills in network monitoring, vulnerability detection, system hardening, and incident response.

Project Objectives

To build technical capacity in cybersecurity and network monitoring among Bhutanese IT professionals to better protect national educational and research infrastructure from emerging cyber threats.

Activities Conducted

Security Fundamentals

Covered types of threats, trends, and basic security measures. Included case studies and policy discussions.

Hands-on Technical Sessions

Demonstrated penetration testing, firewalls, controlled hacking, and configuration risk assessments.

Operational Security Workshops

Focused on system administration, backup policies, continuity planning, and vulnerability response.

Threat-Specific Modules

Addressed malware, phishing, sniffers, DoS attacks, and secure protocols (SSH, SCP, SFTP).

Expert-Led Sessions

Delivered by trainers from Sri Lanka CERT CC and APNIC, including interactive demos and policy design exercises.

Challenges & Future Directions

Challenges Faced

  • Time Constraints: The five-day duration was insufficient for deeper technical implementations and advanced certification content.
  • Diverse Backgrounds: Varied participant experience levels required balancing foundational and technical training content.

Future Initiatives

  • Sectoral CIRT Formation: Establish Computer Incident Response Teams within key sectors
  • Advanced Certifications: Follow-up programs offering deeper security training (TRANSITS, CISSP tracks)
  • Regional Awareness Programs: Basic training campaigns across Bhutanese districts
  • Policy Guidelines: Develop standard cybersecurity guidelines for agency adoption
